The Core Insight

Why Technology comes second — not last

Most AI transformations either obsess over people & process and never ship usable tech, or they buy tech and hope adoption happens. PTP fixes both failure modes.

🎯
People as prime mover
Outcomes, roles, and trust are defined first. Change only sticks when humans own it.
⚡
Tech made tangible fast
Prototypes and demos beat documents. Users see the new workflow in days — not months.
🛡️
Trust by design
Data boundaries, human-in-the-loop controls, and auditability are built in — not bolted on.
♻️
Process as stabilizer
Governance and measurement lock in gains so the organization can scale safely and continuously improve.
The Playbook

3 Phases · 9 Moves

A practical playbook deep enough to include governance and trust, without becoming academic.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑
Phase 1
People — Align
1
Outcome clarity
Define 2–3 measurable outcomes: time saved, quality uplift, risk reduction.
2
Persona + moments that matter
Map where work is painful and where high-value decisions happen.
3
Adoption contract
What leaders, champions, and users commit to — time, behavior, and feedback loops.
⚙️
Phase 2
Technology — Make it Real
4
Use-case to workflow mapping
Identify exactly where AI fits in the flow of work — not beside it.
5
Prototype fast
Demos beat docs. Show the "new way" in days or weeks, not quarters.
6
Trust by design
Data boundaries, transparency, human-in-the-loop, and auditability from day one.
📋
Phase 3
Process — Scale
7
Standardize
"How we do it here" — checklists, runbooks, and templates your team actually uses.
8
Govern + guardrails
Approvals, monitoring, model/tool lifecycle management, and risk controls.
9
Measure + iterate
Adoption metrics + outcome metrics + continuous-improvement cadence.
The Difference

PTP vs. the status quo

Traditional change frameworks were designed for slower technology cycles. The AI wave demands a new ordering.

Dimension Traditional PPT / Change Models PTP Framework
Technology timing Tech comes last (after change is "ready") Tech made concrete in Phase 2 — users see it early
AI adoption stance Abstract promises; "we'll figure out tools later" Demos & prototypes replace slides and theory
Trust & governance Bolted on at the end (or forgotten) Built in from day one (Move 6)
Feedback loop Linear — people → process → maybe revisit Explicit loop: Process feeds back into People
Scale path Process is assumed; rarely explicit Process phase has dedicated governance + measurement moves
The 60-Second Pitch

From problem to proof

Use this narrative when presenting PTP to stakeholders, executives, or investors.

🔴 Problem
Most AI transformations either obsess over people & process and never ship usable tech — or they buy tech and hope adoption happens. Both paths fail, and they fail expensively.
💡 Insight
In the AI era, people need to experience the new workflow early to believe in it — and to guide it. Abstract promises don't drive behavior change. Tangible tools do.
✅ Solution
PTP: People → Technology → Process.
Align humans first. Make the technology tangible fast. Then systematize with process so it scales safely — and keeps improving.
📈 Value
Faster time-to-value. Higher adoption rates. Lower risk — because trust and governance are built in from the start, not added as an afterthought.

The data backs it up: Organizations that prototype early see 3.5× higher ROI (BCG), while excellent change management makes projects 6× more likely to succeed (Prosci).
